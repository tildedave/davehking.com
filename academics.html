My Time in the Academy

I was a graduate student at Penn State University from Fall 2003 to Summer 2009. During that time I had many opportunities to collaborate with some very smart people on topics that I cared passionately about. My advisor was Trent Jaeger.

I defended my thesis ("Retrofitting Programs for Complete Security Mediation") in August 2009.

Graduate school is an interesting career path and in Computer Science we are lucky to be able to follow our passions and still land with decent career prospects. If you are interested in doing research as a career or in working on self-directed projects, I definitely recommend graduate school.

In graduate school, you will be exposed to brilliant people, great ideas, and gain skills that ultimately translate well into real world experience. (That said, it was an incredibly difficult and stressful experience I am happy to have in my rear-view.)
